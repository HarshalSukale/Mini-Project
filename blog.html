Top 5 Tax Saving Tips for 2024

By Harshal | January 5, 2024

1. Invest in ELSS Funds

Equity-Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS) is one of the most popular tax-saving investments in India. These mutual funds offer tax deductions under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, with a lock-in period of just 3 years, the shortest among all tax-saving instruments.

Additionally, ELSS funds have the potential to provide higher returns due to their equity exposure. While they come with a higher risk, the long-term gains can significantly outweigh this.

2. Maximize Your Section 80C Deductions

Under Section 80C, you can claim deductions of up to ₹1.5 lakh by investing in a variety of instruments like PPF, NSC, 5-year fixed deposits, and more. It's essential to plan your investments early in the financial year to make the most of these deductions.

3. Opt for Health Insurance (Section 80D)

Health insurance not only provides financial security during medical emergencies but also offers tax benefits. Under Section 80D, you can claim deductions on premiums paid for health insurance for yourself, your spouse, children, and parents.

The deduction limits are ₹25,000 for individuals below 60 years and ₹50,000 for senior citizens.

4. Save on Rent with HRA

If you are a salaried employee receiving House Rent Allowance (HRA), you can claim tax exemptions on your rent paid, provided you meet certain conditions. Ensure you keep all rent receipts and a copy of the rental agreement as proof.

5. Use the NPS for Additional Savings

The National Pension System (NPS) offers an additional deduction of ₹50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B), over and above the ₹1.5 lakh limit under Section 80C. NPS is a great way to build a retirement corpus while saving on taxes.

By leveraging these tax-saving tips, you can optimize your investments and reduce your tax burden for the year 2024. Always remember to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your individual financial situation.

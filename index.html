Explained: Penalty on Interest Imposed Under Section 234A, 234B, and 234C

Paying income tax and filing income tax returns is the duty of every citizen of India. However, the Income Tax Act also penalizes taxpayers who fail to file their returns on time or who fail to pay their tax dues. Under Sections 234A, 234B, and 234C, taxpayers may be liable to pay interest on late payments and penalties on non-compliance. These penalties can add up, making it crucial for taxpayers to understand these sections and avoid falling into the trap of interest penalties.

Section 194I- TDS on Rent

Section 194-IB of the Income Tax Act, 1961 mentions that tax @ 5% or 3.75% should be.......

Section 194-IB of the Income Tax Act, 1961, states that individuals and HUFs who are not liable to audit under Section 44AB are required to deduct tax at source when paying rent to a resident exceeding ₹50,000 per month. The deduction is typically 5%, but it was reduced to 3.75% during a specific period. This deduction must be done at the time of crediting rent for the last month of the financial year or the last month of tenancy, whichever is earlier.
